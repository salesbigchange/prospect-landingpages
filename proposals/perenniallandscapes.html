Meeting 1: Discovery

Solving the "Software Fatigue"

Attendees: Tom (MD), Alex, Ryan, Brian, Serge.

Perennial has moved from Reflow to ServiceM8 and is looking for a final, robust home for its grounds maintenance division. Key pain points identified:

  • Android Incompatibility: ServiceM8 struggles on your Android devices, frustrating the teams.
  • Invoicing Headache: ServiceM8 invoices per job, but you need to consolidate multiple maintenance visits into one monthly invoice.
  • Quoting Flexibility: You need "bottom line" pricing on quotes without showing the breakdown of labor/materials to clients.
  • Disconnected Systems: Quartix tracking sits outside your job management, creating data silos.

Current State vs. Future State

Moving from disconnected apps to a unified, maintenance-focused platform.

Current State (ServiceM8/Reflow)

  • OS Struggles: ServiceM8 doesn't perform well on Android tablets, limiting hardware choices.
  • Invoicing Pain: Inability to group multiple jobs into a single monthly invoice creates significant admin work.
  • Quoting Limitations: Difficult to present "bottom line" quotes without revealing labor/material splits.
  • Separate Tracking: Quartix tracking is a separate system, meaning you can't easily see "nearest engineer" to a job.

Future State (BigChange)

  • Android Native: Robust Android app ensures your preferred tablets work seamlessly.
  • Consolidated Invoicing: Automatically group all maintenance visits for a client into one clean monthly invoice.
  • Flexible Quoting: "Total Only" quote templates allow you to hide the breakdown and just show the price.
  • Integrated Tracking: Tracking is built-in (free hardware/fitting), allowing live ETA updates and "nearest engineer" dispatch.
